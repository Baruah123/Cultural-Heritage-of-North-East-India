Traditional Dances of Assam

Discover the vibrant and dynamic world of Assam's rich dance traditions.

Bihu Dance

The Bihu dance is one of Assam's most popular folk dances, performed during the Bihu festival. It is characterized by quick hand movements, lively steps, and rhythm that celebrates life and fertility.

The dancers, dressed in vibrant Assamese traditional attire, showcase the rich culture of the region. Bihu is traditionally performed during the Rongali Bihu, which marks the Assamese New Year and the arrival of spring.

Sattriya Dance

Sattriya is a classical dance form of Assam, introduced by the saint Srimanta Sankardev in the 15th century. It originated from the Vaishnav monasteries called 'Sattras'. The dance form is devotional and reflects the cultural and spiritual ethos of Assam.

Costumed in white and adorned with intricate jewelry, the dancers perform with grace, depicting various episodes from the Hindu epics and mythology. Sattriya's rhythm and subtlety make it a distinct classical form that is now gaining worldwide recognition.

Bagurumba Dance

Bagurumba is a traditional dance of the Bodo tribe of Assam. Known for its resemblance to nature, the dance mimics the gentle movements of butterflies and birds. Performed mainly by women, it expresses joy and harmony with the environment.

The dancers wear colorful traditional attire and the choreography is graceful, symbolizing the connection between humans and nature. Bagurumba is usually performed during festivals like Bwisagu, which marks the beginning of the Bodo New Year.
